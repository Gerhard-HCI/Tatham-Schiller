⌬ Dining Etiquette
⚠ Fragrance · Critical

The single most important point at high-end Japanese restaurants. Even a trace of perfume disrupts the chef's work and the experience for other diners at the counter.

  • No perfume or cologne on dining days.
  • No scented hair products — shampoo, spray, styling.
  • No smoking beforehand.
  • Laundry detergent — wash dining clothes in unscented detergent if possible.
  • No strong mints or gum.
Punctuality

At omakase counters the meal begins simultaneously for all guests. Arriving late disrupts the chef's timing for everyone. Allow buffer time — many restaurants are tucked into quiet streets.

Attire

Smart-casual is entirely appropriate; a jacket or tie is not required. A collared shirt is a respectful choice at omakase counters. Comfortable shoes — you will walk a great deal.

At the counter

The hand not holding chopsticks should rest on the table or lightly support a bowl. Sushi counters are often crafted from a single piece of precious wood — please remove rings, bracelets, and watches before sitting.

Tipping

Tipping is not customary and can be awkward. The best appreciation is a sincere "Gochisousama deshita" — "Thank you for the meal." If tipping in other contexts, avoid amounts beginning with 4 — the number is associated with death.

⇄ Getting Around · Suica

Add a Suica commuter pass to your mobile wallet (one per person) — covers subway, bus and train across Tokyo, Kyoto, and most major cities.

  • Open your Wallet app, tap '+', search for Suica under Transit Cards.
  • Load funds — the card appears instantly.
  • Tap your phone at the turnstile gate to pay. Top up via your linked card when low.
¥ Money & Cash

Japan remains a cash-based society. Carry larger amounts of yen — it is entirely common and safe.

  • ATMs at 7-Eleven and Japan Post accept international cards.
  • Most other ATMs do not work with foreign cards.
  • Currency exchange available at airports, hotels and select post offices.
  • Some sushi counters and most pottery studios are cash only.
◐ Connectivity · eSIM

We recommend activating an eSIM before departure. Japan's 4G and 5G coverage is excellent throughout Tokyo and Kyoto.

  • Airalo — competitive rates, wide coverage, easy in-app management.
  • Holafly — unlimited data options, strong for short-term travel.
  • Ubigi — reliable performance, quick smartphone activation.
⚡ Electricity

Japan's voltage is 100V at 50/60 Hz. North American travellers do not need a converter, but sockets are two-pronged — bring a 3-to-2-prong adapter.
